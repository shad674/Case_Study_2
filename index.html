MentorUp

"An application that connects students, who are needing advice and tips in the industry, with mentors who are professionals in their respective fields. Additionally, Mentors are busy and hard to approach, therefore, this app will connect students with the right mentors who have time to coach students.

"I didn't know where to start looking. I mean, I don't feel like I knew a lot of people in the industry." - design student

Background

Nowadays, it is hard to approach upperclassmen or professional workers in their specific fields. People are busy and hard to approach, which makes it harder for students to approach the right mentors. Additionally, Students do not know when and who to approach when it comes to advice and tips in their respective fields. In the end, Students need the right mentors and opportunities to achieve their career goals. They need more than linkedin to know who and when to approach a suitable mentor. The goal of this project is to help lessen STEM student's stress of not getting the right guidance on their career goals.
Problem: How can we help STEM students have the right guidance for their career goals?

Research

As a team, we figured out that college students, more so students in STEM fields do not know know how to approach potentails mentors. As a group, our Cogs 102C main project topic is to create a mobile application that students can use to create a good lasting mentee-mentor relationships with potentails individuals who are respected in their specific fields. We talk to multiple students to check on their career goals and what they wished they have done if they knew how to excel in their fields. We found out that 80% of our interviews that freshman and sophmores mostly know nothing on potentials opportunities in their fields. They start figuring it out when they either join clubs or when people start talking about internships and researches.
"Due to my limited experience coding, it is here where I am unable to bring my hard work to life. If I was paired with a mentor that has developed their coding skills to a considerable extent I think they would serve as a beneficial resource to me" - a Cog Sci Student

Design

the way we approach this problem, is to design an mobile application where students can look for the right mentors, add those mentors to their network, a feature where they can talk to them freely, and a feature where they set up a schedule with the mentors.
